Etsy vs Shopify 2026 —
Which Platform Actually Makes You More Money?

📅 May 2026 ⏱ 12 min read 🇺🇸 US & 🇬🇧 UK fees covered ✍️ ProfitCalcu Team
⚖️

Every handmade seller, print-on-demand shop, and digital product creator faces the same question at some point: should I sell on Etsy, Shopify, or both? The answer is not as simple as "Etsy has fees" or "Shopify is expensive." The right platform depends on where you are in your business, how many sales you make, and how much work you want to put into driving your own traffic.

In this guide, we break down every fee on both platforms for 2026, run the real numbers on a $40 product, compare the pros and cons honestly, and tell you exactly when it makes financial sense to switch — or run both at the same time.

💡 Quick Answer

If you are just starting out, Etsy wins because it brings buyers to you. If you are making consistent sales and want to keep more of your money long-term, Shopify wins on fees — but only once you can drive your own traffic.

Etsy Fees in 2026 — What You Actually Pay

Etsy is not a single fee. It is four separate charges that stack on top of each other every time you make a sale. Most sellers only think about the transaction fee — but by the time all four fees are applied, Etsy is taking a much bigger slice than you might expect.

Here is the full breakdown of Etsy fees in 2026:

1. Listing Fee — $0.20 per item

Every time you list a product on Etsy — whether it sells or not — you pay $0.20. Listings last four months, and they renew automatically when a sale happens. If you sell 10 units of the same item, you pay $0.20 × 10 = $2.00 in listing fees on that one product.

2. Transaction Fee — 6.5% of the sale price

This is Etsy's main revenue cut. It is 6.5% of the total amount the buyer pays — including the item price and shipping. If you charge $40 for a product and $5 for shipping, Etsy's 6.5% applies to $45, not just $40. That comes to $2.93 in transaction fees.

3. Payment Processing Fee — 3% + $0.25 (US)

If you use Etsy Payments (which is mandatory in most countries), you also pay a payment processing fee. In the US, this is 3% + $0.25 per transaction. UK sellers pay 4% + £0.20. This is charged on the total sale amount including shipping and tax.

4. Offsite Ads Fee — 12% or 15% (if applicable)

Sellers making over $10,000/year on Etsy are automatically enrolled in Etsy's Offsite Ads program and cannot opt out. If Etsy promotes your listing on Google, Facebook, or Pinterest and a buyer clicks through and purchases within 30 days, Etsy charges you an extra 12% on that sale. Sellers under $10,000/year pay 15% if they opt in (and can opt out).

⚠️ Important

The Offsite Ads fee applies in addition to all other fees. So on a sale driven by an offsite ad, you could be paying 6.5% + 3% + 12% = 21.5% in fees before listing fee and materials.

The Break-Even Point — When Does Shopify Start Saving You Money?

Shopify's monthly fee is the catch. At low sales volumes, that fixed $29/month cost makes Shopify more expensive overall — even though its per-sale fees are lower. The break-even point is where Shopify's fee savings equal its monthly cost.

Here is how the math works at different monthly sales volumes on a $40 product:

Monthly Sales Etsy Total Fees Shopify Total Fees (incl. $29 plan) Winner

The break-even point falls at roughly 10–12 sales per month at $40 per product. Below that, Etsy's model is actually cheaper in absolute dollar terms because you avoid the monthly platform cost. Above that point, Shopify becomes progressively more economical — and the gap widens quickly as volume grows.

📌 Rule of Thumb

If you are making more than $500/month in consistent Etsy revenue, run the Shopify break-even calculation for your products. At many price points, Shopify starts saving meaningful money at that level.

The Biggest Factor Fees Don't Show — Traffic

Looking at fees alone misses the most important difference between Etsy and Shopify: where your customers come from.

Etsy Brings Buyers to You

Etsy is a marketplace. When someone searches "personalized jewelry gift" or "handmade candle set," Etsy shows them listings from thousands of sellers — and some of those are yours. You are tapping into Etsy's existing audience of over 90 million active buyers who arrive on the platform with a credit card and the intent to buy handmade or unique products.

This is genuinely valuable — especially at the start. You do not need to run ads, build a social media following, or rank on Google to make your first sale on Etsy. You just need a good listing.

Shopify Requires You to Drive Your Own Traffic

Shopify gives you a store, but it does not give you customers. Every visitor to your Shopify store has to come from somewhere — Instagram, Pinterest, Google Search, paid ads, email marketing, or word of mouth. If you cannot generate traffic, you will not make sales, no matter how low your fees are.

This is why Shopify works so well for established sellers and so poorly for brand-new ones. If you already have an Instagram following, an email list, or strong organic Google rankings, Shopify lets you convert that audience at a much lower cost per sale. If you have none of those, you will spend months (and often money) building them before Shopify becomes financially worthwhile.

⚠️ Reality Check

Many sellers who move to Shopify early see their sales drop significantly — not because their products got worse, but because they underestimated how much work goes into driving traffic without Etsy's built-in marketplace.

Etsy vs Shopify — Full Pros and Cons

Etsy — Pros

  • Built-in marketplace traffic — real buyers searching for products like yours every day
  • No monthly fee on the basic plan — you only pay when you sell
  • Fast to set up — you can have a listing live within an hour
  • Trust factor — buyers already know and trust Etsy as a platform
  • Great for handmade, vintage, and digital products — Etsy's categories match these perfectly
  • Built-in reviews system — social proof comes with the platform

Etsy — Cons

  • Higher per-sale fees — roughly 10–12% total, rising to 20%+ with offsite ads
  • You do not own your customers — Etsy owns the relationship, not you
  • Heavy competition — millions of sellers in the same marketplace
  • Algorithm dependency — your visibility depends on Etsy's search rankings
  • Limited branding — your shop looks like every other Etsy shop
  • Policy risk — Etsy can suspend your shop with limited recourse

Shopify — Pros

  • Lower per-sale fees — roughly 3–4% total with Shopify Payments
  • You own the customer relationship — email lists, repeat buyers, retargeting
  • Full brand control — custom domain, design, checkout experience
  • No marketplace competition — your store does not compete with other sellers
  • Scalable — works for $500/month or $500,000/month businesses
  • Better analytics — deeper data on your customers and traffic sources

Shopify — Cons

  • Monthly platform cost — $29–$299/month regardless of sales
  • No built-in traffic — you must market your own store
  • Slower to get started — requires more setup and learning
  • Marketing costs — ads, SEO tools, and email platforms add to the overhead
  • Higher upfront investment — both time and money to get the store right

Which Platform is Right for You?

Choose Etsy if you:

  • Are just starting out and have no existing audience
  • Sell handmade, vintage, craft supplies, or digital downloads
  • Want to test a product idea without upfront investment
  • Make fewer than 10–15 sales per month at your current price point
  • Do not yet have a brand identity or marketing strategy

Choose Shopify if you:

  • Are already making consistent sales (500+/month revenue) and want to keep more of it
  • Have or are actively building a social media audience, email list, or blog
  • Want to build a real brand that customers remember and return to
  • Sell products that are not well-suited to Etsy's categories (e.g., clothing, accessories, home goods)
  • Are frustrated by Etsy's fees, algorithm changes, or shop suspensions

Run Both if you:

  • Want Etsy to keep discovering new customers while Shopify handles repeat buyers
  • Are in the transition phase — growing on Etsy but starting to build your own brand
  • Want to reduce platform dependency risk by not relying on Etsy alone

Running both simultaneously is genuinely the smartest strategy for many sellers at the growth stage. Etsy becomes your acquisition channel; Shopify becomes your retention channel. You direct repeat buyers to your Shopify store with discount codes or packaging inserts, building an email list that Etsy can never take away from you.

Pricing Strategy — Does the Platform Change How You Should Price?

Yes — and this is something most sellers overlook when they compare the two platforms.

On Etsy, you need to price higher to absorb the platform's fees. If your target profit margin is 40%, you need to build Etsy's 10–12% into your price. On a $40 product with $10 in materials and a $29/month Shopify plan equivalent, the math looks very different depending on where you sell.

Use our Etsy pricing formula guide if you want a step-by-step method for setting prices that actually leave you with a profit after all four Etsy fees are paid.

On Shopify, you have more flexibility because your per-sale costs are lower. You could price slightly lower to compete on Google Shopping or run promotions without the fee burden killing your margin. The lower per-sale cost gives you more room to offer discounts and still stay profitable.

The Best of Both Worlds — Using Etsy and Shopify Together

The most successful mid-stage sellers we see do not choose between Etsy and Shopify — they use both strategically. Here is how that model works in practice:

  1. Sell on Etsy to get discovered. Etsy's marketplace brings new customers who have never heard of you. It is your top-of-funnel acquisition channel.
  2. Include a card or flyer in every Etsy order with a discount code for your Shopify store — something like "Get 10% off your next order at [yourshop].com." This moves buyers off Etsy and into your own ecosystem.
  3. Repeat buyers go to Shopify. Once a customer is in your email list or has bookmarked your Shopify store, future purchases happen there — where you keep more of the sale and build the relationship you own.
  4. Use Shopify's email marketing tools to retarget past buyers with new products, seasonal promotions, and loyalty offers. Etsy cannot let you do any of this.

Over time, as your Shopify revenue grows and you build a direct audience, you can reduce your Etsy dependence — or keep both running indefinitely. Many six-figure handmade businesses operate this way.

🏆 Our Verdict — Etsy vs Shopify 2026

For new sellers: start on Etsy. It is lower risk, lower cost at low volumes, and it brings buyers to you. For established sellers making $500+/month: run the numbers with our calculators — at most price points, Shopify saves you meaningful money per sale once you can drive traffic. For the smartest long-term strategy: run both, use Etsy to acquire, and use Shopify to retain and build a brand you own.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Etsy or Shopify better for beginners? +

Etsy is better for beginners because it has built-in traffic and no monthly fee on the basic plan. You can start selling on day one without building an audience. Shopify is better once you have consistent sales and want to reduce fees and own your customer relationships.

Does Etsy take more fees than Shopify? +

On a per-sale basis, yes — Etsy takes roughly 10–12% total while Shopify with Shopify Payments takes around 3–4%. However, Shopify charges a monthly platform fee of $29–$299 which does not apply on Etsy, so at very low sales volumes, Etsy can be cheaper in absolute terms.

Can I use Etsy and Shopify at the same time? +

Yes. Many sellers run both simultaneously — using Etsy for discovery and new customers, and Shopify for repeat buyers and brand building. This is often the smartest strategy, especially in the early and middle stages of your business.

At what revenue should I switch from Etsy to Shopify? +

A rough rule of thumb: if you are making more than $500–$1,000/month consistently on Etsy, it is worth running the numbers for Shopify. At that volume, Shopify's lower per-sale fees often offset the monthly plan cost — especially if you can drive any of your own traffic.

Does Shopify charge a listing fee like Etsy? +

No. Shopify does not charge a per-listing fee. You can have unlimited products in your Shopify store with no extra charge per item. This is one of Shopify's advantages if you have a large catalog — on Etsy, every listing renewal costs $0.20.

Which platform is better for digital products? +

Both work for digital products, but Etsy has a much larger built-in audience actively searching for digital downloads — printables, SVGs, templates, and patterns. For digital products, starting on Etsy makes a lot of sense, and then adding Shopify later as you build an email list and repeat customer base.
