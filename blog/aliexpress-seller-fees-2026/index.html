1. AliExpress Seller Fee Overview 2026

AliExpress operates a commission-based fee model, which means there is no monthly subscription fee and no per-listing fee for international sellers. Instead, AliExpress takes a percentage of each sale you make — the exact percentage depends on which product category you're selling in.

This model is attractive for new sellers because there are zero upfront costs to open a store and list products. You only pay when you earn. However, the combined commission plus payment processing fee adds up to 7% to 11% of your sale price, which is a significant deduction that must be factored into your pricing from day one.

AliExpress distinguishes between two seller types: individual international sellers (most dropshippers and small sellers reading this guide) and Chinese domestic sellers who have a separate fee schedule. This article covers the international seller fee structure applicable to sellers outside mainland China.

📌 Important Note

AliExpress periodically updates its commission rates. The figures in this guide reflect the published 2026 rates for international sellers. Always verify the current rates in your AliExpress seller dashboard before pricing products, especially if you're in a newly added category.

3. Payment Processing Fees

On top of the commission fee, AliExpress deducts a payment processing fee of approximately 2% to 2.5% from each order settlement. This fee covers the cost of handling buyer payments through credit cards, debit cards, PayPal (where available), and the AliExpress Wallet system.

The exact payment processing rate can vary slightly depending on the buyer's payment method and country. Card payments from certain regions (particularly the US, UK, EU) typically settle at the standard 2% rate. Some emerging market payment methods or alternative wallets may carry a slightly higher processing charge.

Crucially, the payment processing fee is applied after the commission has already been deducted — meaning both fees compound slightly against your gross sale amount. In practical terms, the difference is minimal, but it matters when calculating precise margins on low-priced items.

Your net payout per order is calculated as:

4. Listing Fees & Store Setup Costs

One of the most seller-friendly aspects of AliExpress is its zero listing fee policy for international sellers. You can list an unlimited number of products in your AliExpress store without paying per listing — unlike eBay, which charges an insertion fee after your free monthly allowance, or Etsy, which charges $0.20 per listing regardless of whether the item sells.

Store setup on AliExpress is also free. Creating an international seller account, setting up your store page, and publishing products costs nothing. There is no equivalent of an Etsy Plus subscription, an eBay Store subscription, or an Amazon Professional selling plan monthly charge.

However, AliExpress does offer optional paid promotional tools within the platform:

  • AliExpress Sponsored Products — pay-per-click advertising that promotes your listings in search results and category pages. Costs vary by category and competition, typically $0.05–$0.50 per click.
  • AliExpress Choice Programme — invitation-only programme for high-performing sellers that gives products a "Choice" badge and priority placement. No direct fee, but requires meeting specific performance metrics.
  • Coupon & Discount Promotions — sellers can create store coupons (e.g. $2 off $15 spend). The discount cost is borne by the seller and reduces your effective selling price — and therefore your commission base.
  • AliExpress Super Deals & Flash Sales — participation in platform-wide promotions may require offering a minimum discount (typically 20%–30% off). Commission is calculated on the promotional price, not the original price.
📌 Note on Promotional Discounts

When you participate in AliExpress flash sales or platform-wide discount events, your commission is always calculated on the actual price paid by the buyer — not your pre-discount list price. A product listed at $25 and sold at $18 during a promotion will have commission calculated on the $18, not the $25.

5. Shipping Fees — Who Pays What

Shipping on AliExpress works differently depending on your store setup. Most international sellers offer free standard shipping to remain competitive, absorbing the shipping cost into their product price. However, some sellers charge shipping separately and offer multiple shipping speed tiers.

Here is what you need to know about shipping costs and how they interact with your fee calculations:

  • AliExpress Standard Shipping — subsidised logistics service provided by Cainiao (Alibaba's logistics arm). Rates are very low, often $1–$3 per parcel for small items to the US/UK. Transit: 15–30 days.
  • AliExpress Premium Shipping — faster tracked service, $3–$12 per parcel. Transit: 7–15 days to the US/UK. Recommended for customer satisfaction and dispute reduction.
  • AliExpress Choice Shipping — fulfilled from overseas warehouses in the US/EU. $5–$15 per parcel. Transit: 3–10 days. Only available if you have stock in AliExpress's fulfilment warehouses.
  • Third-party couriers (DHL, FedEx, UPS) — fast (3–7 days) but expensive at $15–$40+ per parcel for small items. Used mainly for high-value orders.

Commission is calculated on the total order amount including any shipping charge the buyer pays. If a buyer pays $20 for the product and $5 for shipping, and your category commission is 8%, AliExpress charges 8% on $25 — not just $20. This is an important nuance: if you charge for shipping, you are effectively paying commission on your shipping revenue too.

⚠️ Watch Out

Because commission is charged on the total including shipping, charging separately for shipping doesn't save you fees — it just shifts how the fee is distributed. Many experienced AliExpress sellers choose to offer free shipping and price products to absorb the shipping cost, keeping the transaction cleaner and reducing buyer hesitation at checkout.

For most international sellers, Payoneer or WorldFirst are the most cost-effective withdrawal methods, offering low fees, reasonable exchange rates, and relatively fast processing. If you withdraw large amounts, WorldFirst's tiered fee structure often offers better rates than Payoneer for transactions above $5,000.

7. Dispute & Refund Impact on Fees

AliExpress has a robust buyer protection system — which is good for buyers, but sellers need to understand how disputes affect their fees and revenue.

When a buyer opens a dispute and receives a full refund, AliExpress refunds the commission fee to the seller. The payment processing fee, however, may not always be fully refunded depending on the payment method used and the dispute resolution outcome.

For partial refunds agreed through dispute resolution, the commission is recalculated proportionally on the amount actually retained — so if you agree to a 50% refund on a $20 order, you'll pay commission only on the $10 you keep.

High dispute rates negatively impact your seller performance score on AliExpress, which can lead to reduced search visibility, exclusion from promotional events, and in severe cases, account suspension. AliExpress targets a dispute rate below 1% for top-performing sellers. Maintaining this requires:

  • Accurate product descriptions and photos that match what buyers receive
  • Using tracked, reliable shipping with reasonable delivery timeframes
  • Responding to buyer messages within 24 hours
  • Offering proactive resolution (partial refund or reshipment) before a formal dispute is escalated

10. How to Reduce Your AliExpress Fees

While you can't negotiate AliExpress's commission rates, there are several smart strategies to minimise the effective fee burden on your store:

Price Above Your Break-Even by the Right Amount

Your listing price must always be set so that after deducting commission, payment processing, product cost, and shipping, you still have a meaningful profit margin. A common mistake is pricing at cost-plus-a-small-markup and then being surprised by the fee deductions. Calculate your floor price first: Floor Price = (Product Cost + Shipping) ÷ (1 − Commission% − 2.25%). Set your list price at least 20–30% above floor price.

Sell in Lower-Commission Categories Where Possible

If your product could reasonably sit in multiple categories, test whether a 5% or 6% commission category is a valid fit. Electronics accessories, tools, outdoor equipment, and lighting all carry lower commission rates than fashion or beauty. Mis-categorising products deliberately to cheat fees violates AliExpress policy and can result in account penalties — but choosing the most accurate applicable category is entirely legitimate.

Offer Free Shipping (Absorb Into Price)

Since commission is charged on the total including shipping, offering free shipping simplifies your fee calculation and is preferred by buyers. Build the AliExpress Standard Shipping cost into your product price instead of listing it separately. Free shipping also reduces cart abandonment and dispute rates, both of which protect your long-term margins.

Qualify for AliExpress Choice

AliExpress Choice stores receive priority placement in search results — effectively free organic traffic. This reduces the need to spend on AliExpress Sponsored Products, lowering your effective total cost per sale. Choice status requires consistent high seller ratings (4.7+ stars), fast dispatch times, and low dispute rates over 90+ days of selling history.

Use the Most Cost-Efficient Withdrawal Method

WorldFirst typically offers better FX rates and lower withdrawal fees than international bank wire for large volumes. If you're withdrawing $500+ per month, the difference between a $15 flat wire fee and a 0.5% WorldFirst fee compounds significantly over a year. Review your withdrawal method every quarter and compare current rates.

Time Promotions Strategically

AliExpress major sale events (11.11 Global Shopping Festival, AliExpress Anniversary Sale, Black Friday) drive enormous traffic spikes. Sellers who participate with competitive discounts often 5–10x their normal order volume — and even at 20–30% discounted prices, the volume boost can more than compensate in total monthly profit, even though per-order margin is lower.

11. Is Selling on AliExpress Worth It in 2026?

The answer depends heavily on what you're selling and how you're positioned. Here is an honest assessment:

✅ AliExpress Works Well For

Dropshippers who want to sell globally without holding inventory and without the high FBA fees or advertising spend of other platforms. Niche product sellers who can source unique or hard-to-find items at low cost and command reasonable retail prices. International sellers in countries where domestic e-commerce infrastructure is limited, as AliExpress gives instant access to a global buyer base. Volume sellers in electronics or tools who benefit from the lower 5–6% commission rates.

⚠️ AliExpress Is Harder For

High-margin branded goods — AliExpress buyers expect competitive pricing and will compare you against dozens of similar listings. Sellers who need fast cash flow — the 15-day settlement hold and withdrawal timelines mean you may wait 20–30 days from sale to cash in bank. Premium product sellers — AliExpress's marketplace positioning skews toward value pricing, and premium products can struggle to command premium prices on the platform.

Overall, AliExpress remains one of the lowest-fee selling platforms for international sellers in 2026. With no monthly fee, no listing fee, and a combined take rate of 7–11%, it compares favourably against every major marketplace except Shopify (which requires you to generate your own traffic). The key to profitability is sourcing products at the right cost, pricing correctly from the start, and maintaining a high seller rating to benefit from organic placement.
